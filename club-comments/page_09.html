I also like beating the dead horse because I happen to hate horses and My Little Pony fans. Diablo is released on December 31, 1996; Final Fantasy is released on December 17, 1987 -- there is quite a difference with the dates, don't you think? Sure, both Diablo and Final Fantasy are classics (and crappy games but that's for another discussion) but they are years apart literally. You have to take into consideration the technology between those era: Final Fantasy gets that gray toaster box for a console; Diablo has a giant TV enclosed in a plastic box that can connect to the World Wide Web. We can speculate if Final Fantasy would be a Diablo if Square had access to PC technology.

And as Gonzo mentioned, Tales and Ys destroy the stereotypes of JRPGs. The former's first game is released on the SNES and is a 2D RPG; the latter is Hydlide. Which reminds me: Is Hydlide a WRPG or JRPG???

If anything, "style" can be viewed under a subjective lens. Aesthetics and gameplay techniques can be copied, blurring the distinction between what's western and eastern. I can assure you my favorite RPG series, Wild Arms, might be based on the frontier west, but it has magical girls. Star Ocean 3 is hella JRPG but it's basically made for Trekkies and huge SF nerds. Until the plot twist. Nobody knows what's with that.

This ain't a RPG but the new Intelligent Systems (Advance Wars/Fire Emblem dude) is making this cool ass steampunk game set in The Glorious State of The United States of America and they're basing it on some American comics. Does that mean it's American?

And I could point to Metal Wolf Chaos, a game dubbed in English and very American but was never released to American soil. It's too American to be on American soil.

Which brings back to why I asked this question in the first place: why the distinction between anime and cartoons? And so, does that distinction mean there's a genre?

For me at least, I'm p okay watching little girl cartoons. If Cartoon Network had shows like Aikatsu, I'd watch it. Too bad it doesn't.

I wouldn't say genres are entirely pointless, but a genre based on the country of origin is really hard to place when there are just a million exceptions.
