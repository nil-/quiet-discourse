Kuiper | 06-01-14, 2:57 PM
The hit-and-miss nature of Space Dandy is to be expected from an episodic show with such a wide production staff. (If you look at the credits for season 1, you'll see half a dozen different people credited as "episode director.") Continuity has no meaning in Space Dandy, and it doesn't help that the only fabric holding everything together is a handful of cheap repeat gags that weren't that funny the first time.

That said, the nature of hit-and-miss episodic shows is that you do get your hits among your misses, and the failures are compartmentalized such that the misses don't terribly detract from the hits. I frequently say that one of the things I love to see most on screen creators being given freedom and permission to have fun, and that's certainly present in Space Dandy, even when it's at its worst, which all told actually isn't that bad at all.
