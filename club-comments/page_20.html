Kuiper | 05-11-14, 10:50 AM
I've sunk a significant amount of cash into the 3DS hardware platform. I was an early adopter, I later purchased a 3DS XL, and I also had a capture board installed in my 3DS for video output. I figured that between Fire Emblem, Pokemon, Shin Megami Tensei, and Etrian Odyssey, I'd be rolling in entertainment.

My 3DS is seldom used and I often find myself wondering if perhaps the hundreds of dollars I sunk into it could have been better spent. In general I think the hardware sucks; I bitterly resent the fact that in 2014 I am still being forced to play games at 240p, and paying $40 a pop for the privilege of doing so.

I'm not usually a stickler for graphics, but when you're holding a screen several feet away from your face, pixel density matters. Performance matters, too. It kills me that ten years ago, I was playing Pokemon games that ran at 60 FPS. When I look at my copy of Pokemon Y chugging along at 20 FPS in battles, it makes me want to cry.

The 3DS is among the worst game systems I've ever used and I often find myself resenting the fact that I have to own it in order to follow recent JRPG releases. (I can't help but wonder if I might be enjoying these games more if they weren't burdened by the constraints of the 3DS.) At this point, I'm not even sure it's worth it to keep up. Fire Emblem: Awakening was a disappointment, and Shin Megami Tensei IV did not rock my world. Perhaps I'd enjoy Pokemon more if I were better geographically positioned to participate in VGC.

FWIW, the best time I've had with my 3DS is Mario 3D Land and Phoenix Wright, neither of which are JRPGs. It could just be that I'm done with the genre, but lately the 100+ hours I put into Persona 4 Golden and my continued love for the Dept Heaven series say otherwise.

In related news, anyone who's interested in buying a 3DS with video capture board installed should drop me a line.
