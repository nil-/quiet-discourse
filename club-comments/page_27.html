About Code Geass. We merged the two seasons because having two separate threads for it would be redundant and limiting. It's likely we'll do it for other anime, too, unless in cases with big differences between the seasons. I think we'll definitely do it for the ones that have aired several years ago and that MAL unofficially split because of a hiatus, e.g. Gundam 00

By the way, I'm definitely enjoying Ping Pong. Yuasa's colours and animation make the show fucking delicious. Where most anime limit angle animation, Yuasa's deformed/twisted faces and bodies make it possible to animate bodies and heads in a dynamic and three-dimensional way because the animators don't need to worry about looking realistic. Not needing to look realistic also means he was able to pick atypical but really really satisfying colours for the backgrounds (which also have really good textures while none (or little) of that common "unmoving plead" crap). The unrealistic art style almost makes me want to forgive the somewhat over-the-top scenes.

Some seasonal first impressions, in personal viewing order:

Selector Infected WIXOSS:

From now on, I shall refer to this anime as "Madoka Cards", because WIXOSS is a card game and it is obviously attempting to go down the Madoka route in its approach. Apparently, they are going to make an actual card game based off of this. The problem is, I no have idea what the rules of this game are, since the anime doesn't seem bothered about explaining them, instead preferring to focus screen-time on angst and darkness, as Madoka clones tend to do, which would be fine if I cared about the characters enough beforehand to actually take an interest. Well, at least the soundtrack is cool. A 4-5/10 so far, but the score could easilly go lower or higher than that, since it might actually deliver a surprise or two we don't know about yet.

Kenzen Robo Daimidaler:

An ecchi show disguised as a mecha. Not that anyone would watch it for the mecha design, since Daimidaler has to be the most hideous mecha I have ever seen. Apart from that, it's pretty much all dumb perversion. The only reason the MC even bothers to fend off the enemies is so that he can grope the boobs of the main girl, since doing so produces the "Hi-Ero particles" required to power up Daimidaler. Even the general MAL audience doesn't think this deserves any more than a 6/10, but the penguins and OTT perversion pretty much redeem this as a "so bad, it's good" series.

Ping Pong:

Easily the best thing I am watching, not that the competition is of a high calibre. Since I expect this to be a series focused quite heavily on character interactions and development, it's too soon to say anything other than it looks really good. At least, it does if you don't loathe Taiyou Matsumoto's/Maasaki Yuasa's style, which I assume most here don't.
