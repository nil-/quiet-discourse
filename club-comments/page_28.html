Lindle | 04-11-14, 6:38 PM
Still working on Mushishi's first season, so I haven't picked up Zokushou yet, but other than that here's my ranking of stuff that came out this season.

7th: Black Bullet

The whole damn thing was 90% clumsy exposition, and the remaining 10% is a mixture of Psycho-Pass and Attack on Titan, plus giant zombie insects. Also, the female lead is awful. 3/10

6th: Ryuugajou Nanana no Maizoukin

It started off as a fairly average if well-executed romantic comedy, then started branching out haphazardly into bits of sci-fi and adventure and mystery and it got a bit rushed at the end.

Though I am still surprised this is on the noitaminA block, it has potential to be good. it also has potential to cock up monumentally. One to watch, I guess. 5/10


5th: Soul Eater Not!

Though the redesigns are horrific. I actually enjoyed Soul Eater Not. Mostly it's a good concept for a spinoff, focusing on the day-to-day school life of the students of DWMA instead of the more adventure-based main series. And the setting for Soul Eater is rich enough that it warrants further exploration. The fight scene at the end was also very good. However, I can't say I care too much for any of the new characters. 6/10


4th: Knights of Cydonia

So, Knights of Sidonia was interesting. I won't say "good", because really, it's too soon to tell. But it's definitely interesting. I was surprised at first, because there was a lot of dialogue and actual exposition, and this is an adaptation of a Tsutomu Nihei manga - he isn't exactly known for either of those two things.

Once you get past those conventional aspects, though, it's still pretty unconventional - not counting the full-CG art which isn't as distracting as you'd expect. It immediately builds up a dense setting, raises a lot of questions, and throws in some fascinating sci-fi concepts like the development of a third gender.

It's pretty obvious why this of all Tsutomu Nihei manga finally got adapted, though. After a long period of peace in hiding from a humungous race that tries to kill them, suddenly they are under attack again? SIE SIND DAS ESSEN UND WIR SIND DIE BANDWAGON.

6/10


3rd: Haikyuu!

Simple but effective, and a noticeable improvement on the manga by means of better pacing, good directing, and art that is up to a standard where I can actually fucking recognize one character from another. 7/10


2nd: Ping Pong the Animation

The MAL threads for it make me want to choke infants.

Anyway, Ping Pong is pretty much all presentation at the moment, but the presentation is fucking perfect. Taiyo Matsumoto and Yuasa Masaki are just about the most perfect Mangaka/Director combination ever conceived, with their respective styles being highly distinctive and more or less equivalent to one another.

Combine this with great pacing, dialogue, and voice acting (seriously the Chinese in this is great), plus some fantastic animation, and you've got Ping Pong. 8/10
