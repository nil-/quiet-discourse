Mushishi had a pretty great first episode, and JoJo is off to a great start as well. Mahouka might be interesting, I mean the setting isn't original at all but it could work providing it doesn't fall victim to the various number of cliches that it could so easily fall to. Matsutarou surprised me a lot actually especially given all the negative backlash it's received so far. It's pretty good! A very fun show. Matsutarou reminds me of Pete from pretty much any Disney short with his mischief and I think it'll be interesting to watch him (potentially) change as a person. It's interesting to see a show that's about sumo wrestling too, especially one by Tetsuya Chiba - it kind of seems like a somewhat lighthearted version of Ashita no Joe. Sumo wrestling looks fun :))))))

Akuma no Riddle is pretty weak. It's pretty dark and edgy with mediocre animation, if there's actual yuri within the next couple of episodes then I might stick with it.

Of course I'm still waiting for Ping Pong to blow everything out of the water.

Stellio | 04-09-14, 9:31 AM
If I had to be frank, Haikyuu is what I think to be Production I.G.'s foothold to making some more $$$. I mean, with Kuroko no Basket's great success, and the fact that Haikyuu is going to be another adaptation with some already dedicated fans, it's kinda hard not to see it that way. I love me some sports anime nonetheless, and I bet other studios will catch on over time and, for instance, make an anime about Ping Pong or something.

However, I do theorize time to time that this is merely Production I.G bulking up some extra cash. And eventually, I hope they dedicate their time to making something new (non-sports related, in other words). I mean, honestly, Kuroko no Basket and even Haikyuu's first episode weren't bad at all (I rlly like KnB as well), but next thing you know, a golf ball will fly out of Earth's atmosphere only to pierce right through a NASA facility on its way down, but then, getting intercepted by an airplane, finally lands right in the hole for a birdie, or something. Oh yeah, and all done in a Elementary School tournament.

Most shows have a lot of potential this season, like Bokura wa Minna Kawaisou, Isshuukan Friends, and Nisekoi. But it's really a mixed bag. My most anticipated shows are both airing on the 11th so, yeah.

Also, Dolphy pls. Gokukoku no Brynhildr probably isn't gonna be worth your time to argue against, unless you want to re-watch Elfen Lied as well (considering how bad your memory is). I have the same problem with Gokukoku as another excellent anime called Strike Witches: there're cute girls and a bigger, grander narrative, but they are very, very disconnected. And most of the time, the latter will be ignored in lieu of the focus on the cute girls. And, with Strike Witches, dat focus is on the arse, while Gokukoku on an eventual, histrionic romance drama that isn't really original or interesting. In other words, don't watch it unless you want a half-baked show with half-baked Sci-Fi stuff and cute girls + half-baked romance.

henriiez | 04-09-14, 6:53 AM
I guess I'll do my thoughts on the Spring 2014 Season as well along with an initial score.



Bokura wa Minna Kawaisou - I felt that this was just going to be the next Sakurasou no Pet na Kanajo with more shiny colours. For those who don't know what Sakurasou is about, it's about a group of unique people all living in a dorm. (6/10)

Blade and Soul - This didn't feel interesting nor did it feel like the characters were moving as if it were their actions. It felt more like certain words and actions had to be fulfilled so they just did so, but seeing as this is based off a game. It didn't surprise me. (5/10)

Selector Infected WIXOSS - The concept of the anime of the anime is weird seeing as being able to continuously win a card-game they will become a person who will be able to make wishes come true but if they lose 3 times, they will lose that priviledge. (5/10)

Mushishi Zoku Shou - Erm... Awesome! It's back guys, Rejoice! (9/10)

Akuma no Riddle - I feel like this is going to be one of those weird anime where nothing makes coherent sense yet the anime tries to make it seem that way. It was like a mix between Danganronpa and Medaka Box. (4/10)

Seikoku no Dragonar - Mixed feelings towards this anime. I don't hate it, on the contrary, I liked it but it was no very good of an anime. The concept of the anime seemed a little off as well being that it's about people summoning dragons yet the protagonist summons a girl. Oh, how typical of anime. Reminded me a bit of Dog Days. (5/10)

Kamigami no Asobi - Well, looks like there's another reverse harem... with the gods? And a shrine maiden? And...? Wah? At least they won't, I hope, have as many males as Brother Conflict. At least it's not incest this reverse harem time around. (4/10)

Mahouka Koukou no Rettousei - The concept is interesting but is kind of resticted and ruined by the school setting. Also, the protagonist -- aka the brother, also made friends exceptionally quickly without having to do a single thing. I feel that the anime would flow and be so much more interesting if they stayed away from school. (6/10)

Captain Earth - My hatred of the mecca genre doesn't shine through as it would some of the other anime of the same genre, even one I dropped purely because I saw some kind of robot (from this season). Not too sure about this one, but props for making me watch the whole episode and not having a cringe fest. Not too sure about the score for this one because it's influenced by my surprise of getting through the episode. (7/10)

Soredemo Sekai wa Utsukushii - I had to think about what this anime felt like. It felt a bit like Romeo and Juliet; but with four daughters on the other side whilst the "Romeo" side of things were that of the norm. Curious to see how they develop this anime further and the interactions between the couple which seem to be soon wedded. I hope they don't forget about the characters that appeared in the first episode. (8/10)

Haikyuu!! - My god. Production IG really knowns how to make you pumped for an anime. I think this anime will treat as well in the abscence of Kuroko's Basketball since he needs half-year breaks only to go for another half-year. (8/10)

Gokukoku no Brynhildr - Unsure what this anime was trying to do with it's first episode but it had scenes that I could say I genuinely enjoyed. As for the rest, having the protagonist (try?) to devote his live in order to prove aliens exist in his childhood-friend's place since she died seemed kind of unnatural. I'd also wish they'd seriously stop with the time-skips where the characters are old enough to be in high-school. (6/10)

Baby Steps - I'm curious to see how the relationship will or won't develop between the two MC's. I like the concept of the studious person learning something in a way which normally wouldn't be done, especially by themselves at the age he is. Other than that, I'm a fan of sports anime so I get compelled to watch anime that's related to any type of sport. This season.. so much sport. (6/10)

Isshuukan Friends. - This is my cup of tea. I don't think there has been an anime that has one of the MC's constantly forgetting things, well, at least not in the same manner as this. The art and overall feel of the anime is remeniscent that of Usagi Drop. I think this may be one of those anime that isn't particularly good but as a special place where it shines within me. Or, it is very good and I will love it. (7/10)

Love Live! School Idol Project 2nd Season - I'm not quite sure why I'm watching this. I'm gone. I'm blank. I have nothing to say. (5/10)

Mangaka-san to Assistant-san to the Animation - Just a kick-back anime because it's half the duration of the average episode. (6/10)

Kanajo ga Flag wo Orarctara - Looks like this anime won't make much coherent scence seeing as a girl feel down one floor and her death was prevented by her falling in love because our, oh so special, protagonist kisses her. Not to sure what to think about the two characters that seem to be the focal point of the anime since there does seem to be some chemisty between them but it's lacking something. (5/10)

Black Bullet - I didn't like the way they executed the first episode. I wonder how it's possible to even try to fit all the mumbo-jumbo into one episode to try to explain the situation and everything else whilst trying to do it in a round-about way. If they weren't so blatantly obvious if trying to force feed us information, I would have liked it. Potentially a four, but between that and five, I chose five. (5/10)

Soul Eater Not! - Just as the name implies. It's not Soul Eater. Although some characters from the parent anime are in it, the art and the way everything was presented was kind of out of place. Maybe I was too focused on how it differed from the parent anime. The preview for the next episode doesn't look like the anime will go anywhere soon. (5/10)

Mahouka Koukou no Rettousei - this season's overhyped and overrated blandfest. probably the dullest first episode of the season, and the main character and his sister's relationship just should not be a thing.
Mahou Shoujo Taisen - no idea what happened in this. it looked like it would have beautiful animation, but it didn't. it was retarded.
