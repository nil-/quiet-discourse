From Nasuverse, I love Fate/Stay Night (the VN), I feel it has a lot of interesting characters (Shirou in particular is awesome), I like the existence of sex scenes - I think almost everything with a fairly serious tone should have them - even if the writing in them could use some work, and I automatically adore any character who I see in both heroic fantasy battles and daily cooking life. The much-maligned sex and cooking scenes both added to it for me, and I loved the whole experience overall.

Tsukihime I feel is less focused and less well-delivered, but has some interesting atmosphere and I thought it was alright.

The anime for both I haven't seen, but I have heard nothing but bad things. Even people who liked it generally mention that it was bad but they liked it anyway.

Fate/Zero I think has some interesting Nasuverse characters and plot elements (I like the whole structure of the Holy Grail War, and I find Kotomine and to a lesser extent Kiritsugu to be interesting characters). Like Tsukihime I feel it doesn't come together, and many of the characters don't have arcs that end in impactful ways. I also think the examination of Kiritsugu's moral system is much less interesting than the parallel examination it receives in the Fate/Stay Night VN.
