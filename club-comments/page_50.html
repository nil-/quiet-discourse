lpf | 02-10-14, 5:51 PM
The protagonists fall from somewhere, the female character's breasts ending up pressing on the main character's face. The characters didn't fall for good or necessary reasons- a few seconds are used to show the main character in that enviable position, and five more seconds are used to show both characters blushing with the boy apologising profusely and hiding in the closest hole. What can we say about that? Well, first of all that this kind of pandering potentially detracts from the pacing and plotting, and in many cases sacrifices other elements to earn easy audiences (one could make the same criticism of sex scenes in some visual novels). The main case I would not count the waste of time and such as a con is when it is in the context of a legitimate joke in a consistently comedic show, and consistently erotic too, maybe. But my example in itself is neither clever comedy nor clever eroticism.

A character sits on her bed, talking on the phone to a friend's child. When the call is over, she crashes on the bed lying, as she ponders about her conversation. In the movement, her light clothes reveal her chest for half a second. Another situation. Robot pilots wear futuristic suits to enter the robots, but these suits happen to be skin-tight and very erotic on female pilots. What happens? In both situations, no time is lost, and the script isn't derailed, either. If it doesn't matter either way, why not sex it up? Of course there are several criticisms that could be made. In some contexts, it would not feel appropriate (e.g. sexualising the daughter in an anime about parenting and family bonds would be a bad idea- and in a similar line of thought the fanservice in Sora no Oto cound be considered intrusive). In any context, one could find it distasteful, for many reasons... While I do think that this kind of fanservice can be unwanted sometimes, I can say that I am prone to accepting it, or at times enjoying it!

A show concludes every episode with the main character relaxing at the end of the day, with relaxing music in the background, and her giving her afterthoughts about what happened. Element of interest, all of these scenes happen in the bathtub, and you can almost see her naked body through the water. Could have it been another way? Yes, but at the same time, having these scenes in the bathtub with the steam and all adds to the relaxing vibes of these scenes and gives a setting more ritual and more credible (at least for every episode) for introspection than simply cooking or falling asleep.

A show gives the most trite reason to go to hot springs, and uses it to have many suggestive scenes. Potentially, somewhere in the episode one of the characters will break the fourth wall and make a joke about how the onsen episode is obligatory. In essence, this kind of cliché reflects the authors giving up on originality and cleverness to give the fans more smut and keep or gain the viewership. Yet, these episodes don't necessarily affect pacing/plotting in slice-of-life or romance shows and can be minor issues- even Aria has an onsen episode (although a point could be made about how it is in line with the soothing atmosphere of the series in general). But people will remember the Muv-Luv anime with its hot spring episode...

And lastly, a female character flirting with the main character, because the latter is handsome plus he's helped the girl overcome her issues. She uses suggestive body language and the camera shots are accordingly suggestive. She pets the protagonist's thigh, bites his ear... Sexual? Definitely. Appropriate? Enjoyable? Hell yeah.
