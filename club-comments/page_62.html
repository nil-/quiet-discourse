twinbells | 01-27-14, 5:54 PM
I feel like Lain and Haibane differ significantly in terms of how they explore their respective settings. Lain has this poorly-realized speculative science fiction aspect where it touches on all kinds of weird random shit like techno-drugs, telepathic kids, and crystallized onee-chans. I personally found myself not liking the series as much after a couple rewatches due to how completely unhinged it sometimes got, with both stand alone-ish episodes like the virtual reality episode, and the later infodumping episodes (KNIGHTS OF THE EAAAASTER CALCULUS) being particularly bad. (That cat sweater tho... 10/10)

Haibane maintains the strong atmospheric qualities of Lain, but otherwise uses a completely different set of storytelling techniques. The setting is established through the perspective of someone who is experiencing all of the aspects of daily life for the first time so its narrative is much more grounded in nature. When Haibane gets more abstract I feel that it doesn't quite lose its focus in the same way that Lain does.
