Traditional mahou shoujo titles (include similar terminology), to most ~anime experts~ and me, are shows that don't involve a lot of fighting. The first magical girl shows and manga such as Sally the Witch and Himitsu no Akko-chan were shows about girls who can change clothes and help people out. Literally. The live-action movie trailer for Akko-chan exemplifies this.

Sailor Moon started the trend where magical girls started fighting. A bunch of Western anime experts (i.e. some dudes on Wikipedia) argue Sailor Moon started moving magical girls to sentai. So we get works that look like your Mighty Morphin' Power Rangers like the Precures.

We still get traditional magical girl shows every now and then. The Jewelpet franchise is a great contemporary example. So traditional magical girl shows do exist and that definition won't go away for a while until anime is really dead.

I don't think any one of us have watched a lot of those. Even as a little girl cartoon expert, I am afraid to say that I haven't either :-(

nil- | 12-18-13, 8:28 PM
Was asked on my profile but I'll answer it here in case anyone else is wondering the same thing.
boy's hater said:
What happens to the anime/manga/discussions that are "polled"? I know they get archived, but are they in the "relations list" on the right side of the club at all? And is there going to still be a system of weekly voting? Other words, how do polls work? I'm just a bit confused on these points, pls clarify. thx, qt.

There will be a system of weekly voting: 5 questions as seen in the example poll, based on last week's discussion. Then the voting results get put into the Archive. That's it. There will not be any relations list, no comparisons to a certain minimum standard will be asked for in discussion, and there are no cutoff percentages or numerics for anything. The voting results serve many purposes, and with the more directly implied assumptions attached to them.

For example, one can take the 10 highest scored titles in the Archive and call it the club favorites. The sample size, poll date, and standard deviation of people's scores tell a more complicated and thus a more realistic tale; infer as you'd like as to what you believe the club "endorses" (officially, it's nothing). You can also presume, say, anything higher than 7.50 is a good title to recommend to others, and thus use the Archive that way. It's also an excellent history collection of the club's discussions, and if for example a title goes up again in another thread, it'll be another entry onto the Archive. Then you can compare the voting results of it now compared to the voting results of it in the past.

In short, the Archive is a fat bunch of data, and you can get creative and do whatever type of inferring you'd like with it. It does more than one thing well.
